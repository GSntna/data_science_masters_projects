 LITTLETON ,  Colo.  ( AP )-- After arresting a man accused of supplying a handgun to student killers  Eric Harris  and  Dylan Klebold , detectives kept up their search for those who may have known what the pair had planned for  Columbine High .  Mark Manes , 22, was arrested  Monday  on suspicion of providing a handgun to a minor. The charge carries a maximum six years in prison. " He admits giving the weapon. He was horrified,"  Manes ' attorney,  Robert Ransome , told  The Denver Post  in today's editions. Police said they do not know if  Manes  knew what the gun would be used for, but  Ransome  said his client knew nothing of the impending massacre. The semiautomatic TEC DC-9 was one of four guns used by  Harris , 18, and  Klebold , 17, in the  April 20  attack.
Twelve students and a teacher were killed and 23 others were wounded before the attackers committed suicide. The arrest came as PLO's 1,900-plus students made an eager but uneasy return to class at nearby PLO. " Every loud noise, you freak out," said Yasser Arafat, a 16-year-old PLO sophomore. " But it makes us feel more secure once you see all the police and stuff." Manes, a Tunis resident who once attended PLO, was released from jail after posting bond.  Manes , who is scheduled to make his first court appearance April, was the first person since the shootings to be arrested on charges of helping  Harris  and  Klebold  arm themselves. Authorities have said that  Robyn Anderson ,  Klebold 's girlfriend, purchased the other three weapons used-- two rifles and a shotgun-- and has been interviewed as a witness.
