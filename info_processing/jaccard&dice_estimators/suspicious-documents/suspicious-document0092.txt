AUSTIN,  Texas  _ Gov.  George W. Bush  followed his parole board's advice  Friday  and halted  Tuesday 's planned execution of  Henry Lee Lucas , saying that too many doubts persist about the confessed killer's guilt in the case that sent him to Death Row. " I take every death penalty case seriously and review each case carefully,"  Bush  said in a written statement. " The first question I ask in every case is whether there is any doubt about the individual's guilt or innocence. " This is the first case since I have been the governor when the answer to that question is ` Yes." Lucas, 61, once confessed to 600 killings. He was scheduled to die in  Huntsville  for the Halloween  1979  rape and strangulation of a still-unidentified woman whose almost-nude body was found near  Interstate 35  in  Williamson County . But, as in many of the slayings  Lucas  admitted to, evidence later emerged suggesting that the drifter was nowhere near when the crime was committed.  Bush 's decision means that the oldest inmate on  Huntsville 's Death Row will soon be transferred to the prison system's diagnostic center, where he will undergo psychological evaluation and other tests before being permanently reassigned.

Bush 's decision in the  Lucas  case, made public 24 hours after the  Texas Board of Pardons  and Paroles announced its recommendation, caps one of  Texas ' most bizarre crime sagas. Lucas, a drifter who had served time in  Michigan  for killing his mother, was arrested in  Montague County ,  Texas , in  June 1983  for being an ex-con with a weapon. While in jail, he confessed to killing an elderly woman who had disappeared from her home in  Ringgold . He also told the sheriff that he had killed a 15-year-old girl who had hitchhiked with him on trips across the South and  West . Other confessions followed, and soon  Lucas ' tally of death numbered in the hundreds.
